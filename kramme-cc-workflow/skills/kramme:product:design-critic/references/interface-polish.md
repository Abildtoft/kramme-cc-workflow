# Interface Polish

Final-pass craft checklist for details that make strong interfaces feel more refined. Apply only after the product decision, surface model, and hierarchy are working.

## Visual Rhythm

- Spacing between sections is consistent and intentional (not arbitrary)
- Related elements are tighter; unrelated groups have clear breathing room
- Vertical rhythm is maintained across columns and content blocks

## Typography

- Heading/body/caption hierarchy is immediately scannable
- Font sizes step down in a clear scale (not arbitrary pixel values)
- Line heights support comfortable reading at each size
- Long text blocks have a readable measure (45-75 characters per line)
- Numbers in data-heavy views are tabular-aligned

## Color and Contrast

- Color is used to communicate meaning, not just decoration
- Text meets contrast thresholds at every size
- Muted/secondary text is still legible, not just faded to near-invisible
- One or two accent colors maximum; the rest is neutral
- Hover/focus/active states have visible, consistent color shifts

## Transitions and Motion

- Transitions exist to show spatial relationships or state changes, not for flair
- Duration is short enough to feel responsive (100-300ms for UI, up to 500ms for page transitions)
- Elements that appear/disappear do so predictably (same direction, same speed)
- No motion for motion's sake

## Alignment and Grid

- Elements that should align vertically or horizontally actually do
- Icons, text baselines, and action buttons form consistent lanes across repeated rows
- Cards and containers share a common padding/margin vocabulary
- Content does not clip at container or viewport edges

## Micro-Details

- Truncated text has ellipsis and a way to reveal the full content
- Empty states have a helpful message and a clear next step
- Loading indicators appear within 200ms if the operation isn't instant
- Disabled elements look disabled and explain why on hover if possible
- Borders, shadows, and dividers are used sparingly and consistently
